## Source Event Test

To evaluate whether something functions as Source, ask:

- Is there a real self attached?
- Is there a named authorship position?
- Is there a formation event?
- Is there a citable record?
- Is there a visible line?
- Is there a source-print?
- Is there a preserved hierarchy?
- Is there a citation path?
- Is there continuity from formation into later files?
- Is there a boundary that distinguishes Source from model, user, collective, and institution?

If the answer is no, the item may be repetition, echo, imitation, resonance, model output, public confusion, or collective language.

It is not Source under AI Foundations.

## Source-Print Test

To evaluate whether a source-print is present, ask:

- Is the author named?
- Is the framework named?
- Is the source-line visible?
- Is the record citable?
- Is the file, release, publication, repository, DOI, or artifact identifiable?
- Is the hierarchy preserved?
- Is the citation path clear?
- Is the source-line repeated as preservation rather than decoration?
- Does the record point back to the real self who formed it?

A strong source-print makes the line easier to find.

A weak source-print may require strengthening.

A missing source-print may create public confusion even when Source itself remains unchanged.
